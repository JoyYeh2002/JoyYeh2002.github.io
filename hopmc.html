Who are We?

When COVID-19 brought our high school career to a sudden halt, many new JHU students embraced their favorite videogame and childhood memories - Minecraft. This simple yet fascinating sandbox game is like LEGO but digital; one can build virtually anything: from skyscrapers to automatic farms to custom-coded game extensions.

Several friends and I from JHU c/o 2024 were meeting new friends on a multi-player Minecraft server. From its growing popularity, we saw the game's incredible power to connect and unite people, especially under the distress of a pandemic. In the summer of 2020, we started HopMC, the official JHU student group for Minecraft.

The Adventure

After playing on a survival server for a month, about 15 players became interested in re-building the Johns Hopkins Homewood Campus in the game. Starting on August 6th, 2020, our epic build project began.

We started with Gilman Hall, the most distinctive building on campus with a clock tower, an archeology museum, and a large reading room in the back. We based the scale off of several blueprints found on Google - which turned out to be very accurate.
